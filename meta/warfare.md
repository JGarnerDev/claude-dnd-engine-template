# Warfare

DM-side design list of gameplay aspects and mechanics with a military dimension. This is a palette and tracking index, not a rules document — when an item matures into a table-runnable mechanic, it graduates to its own file in `meta/mechanics/` (built from `_template.md`) and the entry here keeps a wikilink pointer.

Player-sourced war desires (guerrilla resistance, gorilla warfare, invasion beats) live in [[campaign-design-preferences]] — that file tracks what the group wants to *experience*; this one tracks how war actually *plays* at the table.

**Status line:** each item carries one of `idea` (sketch only), `drafted` (notes exist, not playable), `mechanic-built` (graduated to `meta/mechanics/`, link it).

## Mass Combat & Battles

- **Pitched battle resolution** — how a full army-vs-army engagement resolves without grinding the table through hundreds of rolls. Party actions at key points swing the larger battle.
  - *Status: idea*
- **Battlefield set pieces** — the party fights a normal-scale encounter embedded in a larger battle; the war rages around them as moving terrain, hazards, and shifting objectives.
  - *Status: idea*
- **Siege** — attacking or defending a fortified position over days or weeks. Supply, morale, walls, and sortie opportunities. Could chain with [[clock-system]] for siege progression.
  - *Status: idea*

## Command & Leadership

- **Commanding troops** — party members lead units rather than fight alone. Orders, unit morale, and the cost of spending soldiers' lives.
  - *Status: idea*
- **Unit creation** — players build their own military units: hand-pick individuals (named NPCs, recruits with personalities and quirks), decide the unit's focus (skirmishers, shock troops, scouts, siege specialists), discover and choose what equipment to outfit them with, and build the unit's renown through deeds. The unit becomes a persistent party asset with its own identity, growth arc, and losses that hurt. Recruitment ties to [[home-base]]; outfitting ties to [[crafting]] and the war economy.
  - *Status: idea*
- **War council** — strategic decision scenes: where to commit forces, which front to abandon, who to trust with command. Social pillar wearing a uniform.
  - *Status: idea*
- **Faction war state** — extends [[faction-leadership]]: what changes when a faction the party leads or belongs to goes to war.
  - *Status: idea*

## Logistics & Strategy

- **Supply lines** — armies eat. Food, horses ([[campaign-design-preferences]] crop-failure beat can chain here), and materiel as strategic targets and constraints.
  - *Status: idea*
- **War clocks** — front lines advancing, cities falling, reinforcements arriving — modeled as background clocks the party's missions speed up or slow down. Builds on [[clock-system]].
  - *Status: idea*
- **Intelligence & scouting** — knowing where the enemy is as a resource. Recon missions, spies, counter-intelligence, the cost of acting on bad information.
  - *Status: idea*

## Irregular Warfare

- **Occupation & resistance** — the party operates inside enemy-held territory. Safe houses, sympathizers, reprisals against civilians as a consequence dial. Directly serves the player-sourced *assassinations and guerrilla warfare* desire in [[campaign-design-preferences]].
  - *Status: idea*
- **Sabotage missions** — destroy the bridge, burn the granary, spike the siege engines. Heist structure with military stakes.
  - *Status: idea*
- **Raids & ambushes** — small-unit actions: hit fast, withdraw before the response arrives. Tension between greed (push further) and discipline (stick to the plan).
  - *Status: idea*

## Aftermath & Texture

- **War weariness & morale** — how prolonged conflict changes towns, prices, NPCs, and recruitment. The war is visible even in sessions that aren't about it.
  - *Status: idea*
- **Veterans & the wounded** — NPCs and hirelings shaped by battles the party fought or caused. Consequences with faces.
  - *Status: idea*
- **War economy** — mercenary contracts, profiteering, arms trade. Money pillar of war; ties to routes and resource scarcity.
  - *Status: idea*
