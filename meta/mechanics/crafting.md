# Crafting

Player-driven creation of weapons and items. Tied to the home base — the base provides stations and infrastructure; crafting gives those stations purpose. Covers both weapon crafting and general item crafting.

## Overview

- **Encounter type:** crafting
- **Session fit:** short | downtime
- **Difficulty:** scalable
- **Player count:** any

## Design Intent

Players want ownership over their gear. Crafting should feel like mastery — not just a resource sink, but a way to express character identity through what they make. Weapon crafting specifically should have mechanical weight (properties, materials, time invested).

## Dependency

Requires home base with at least a basic crafting station. See [[home-base]].

## Scope

- **Weapon crafting** — forging, modification, or enhancement of weapons. Material choice matters; time investment matters.
- **Item crafting** — mundane and magical goods. Potions, tools, armor, consumables.

## DM Notes

- Crafting should never feel like busywork — each item made should have a plausible use or story value
- Rare materials should be sought, not bought — the resource hunt is part of the experience. Draw from the resource palette in [[worldbuilding]] (60+ entries, common to unique arcane) rather than inventing materials ad hoc
- Don't over-complicate the first crafting interaction; ease players into the system
- Each PC should have a crafting hook tied to their identity, class, or backstory — the system should feel personally relevant to every player, not generic infrastructure
