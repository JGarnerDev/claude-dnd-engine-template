# Crafting

Player-driven creation of weapons and items. Tied to the home base — the base provides stations and infrastructure; crafting gives those stations purpose. Covers both weapon crafting and general item crafting.

## Overview

- **Encounter type:** crafting
- **Session fit:** short | downtime
- **Difficulty:** scalable
- **Player count:** any

## Design Intent

Players want ownership over their gear. Crafting should feel like mastery — not just a resource sink, but a way to express character identity through what they make. Weapon crafting specifically should have mechanical weight (properties, materials, time invested).

## Dependency

Requires home base with at least a basic crafting station. See [[home-base]].

## Scope

- **Weapon crafting** — forging, modification, or enhancement of weapons. Material choice matters; time investment matters.
- **Item crafting** — mundane and magical goods. Potions, tools, armor, consumables.

## DM Notes

- Crafting should never feel like busywork — each item made should have a plausible use or story value
- Rare materials should be sought, not bought — the resource hunt is part of the experience. Draw from the resource palette in [[worldbuilding]] (60+ entries, common to unique arcane) rather than inventing materials ad hoc
- Don't over-complicate the first crafting interaction; ease players into the system
- Each PC should have a crafting hook tied to their identity, class, or backstory — the system should feel personally relevant to every player, not generic infrastructure

## Incremental Augmentation (proposed — DM to approve)

Rather than replacing gear with loot drops, players incrementally improve a signature weapon or armor piece over the campaign. The item grows with the character — each augment is a story beat, not a shopping trip.

- **Augment slots** — an item holds a limited number of augments (2–3 to start); slots expand with crafting station tier or campaign milestones
- **Tiered enhancements** — minor (flavor/utility) → moderate (+1, rider effects) → major (signature property). Each tier demands rarer materials, higher DCs, and more downtime
- **Material defines the augment** — the resource chosen determines what the enhancement does. Draw from the resource palette in [[worldbuilding]]; the hunt for the material is the quest hook
- **Station gating** — augment tiers gate on home base crafting station tier (see [[home-base]]), so base progression and gear progression feed each other
- **Signature items become entities** — once an item carries augments, it earns its own entity file and history; each augment is canonized as part of the campaign record
- **Per-PC hook** — each PC's signature item should tie to their identity or backstory, per the DM note above

Why: rewards attachment over churn. Loot shifts from "better sword" to materials and components, and players keep the weapons their stories are written into.
