# Religion & Cult Building

Players (and NPCs) can found cults, build faiths, recruit followers, and write doctrine. Religions emerge from play — not just as background scenery but as live political and spiritual actors. Gods are real and present; a faith built earnestly can be answered.

## Overview

- **Encounter type:** social | exploration | any
- **Session fit:** long-term campaign arc; individual faith-building moves happen in any session
- **Difficulty:** scalable
- **Player count:** any

## Design Intent

Religion in this world is a live mechanic, not background flavor. The same system that lets players build a faction lets them build a faith — but faith adds layers faction doesn't: doctrine, miracles, the possibility of genuine divine contact, and the specific danger of a god noticing you. NPCs use this system too; cults can emerge as consequences of player actions.

## Pillars

- **Founding** — establishing the faith's identity: a name, a god or concept, a core doctrine (even if loose), a founding act or symbol
- **Recruitment** — drawing followers through deeds, rhetoric, location, or desperation; followers have beliefs and can leave
- **Doctrine** — what the faith teaches; doctrine creates expectations and can be weaponized by enemies or corrupt insiders
- **Infrastructure** — shrines, temples, clergy; physical presence makes a faith legible and targetable
- **Divine attention** — gods are real; a growing faith may attract its patron's notice, which can mean blessing, demand, or complication

## Relation to Faction Leadership

A religion is a kind of faction with additional layers. See [[faction-leadership]] for the underlying framework — religion builds on it with doctrine, divine mechanics, and the specific volatility of belief.

## Tensions

- A faith grows fastest when it answers a need; the DM controls what the world is hungry for
- Doctrine can be written by founders, corrupted by successors, or weaponized by enemies
- Miracles create expectations; expectations create obligations
- Other factions (guilds, nobles, rival faiths) will resist or co-opt a growing religion

## DM Notes

- Introduce faith-building as an option, not an assignment — players who want it will find it; don't push
- NPCs founding cults in response to player actions is a realistic world consequence; use it
- Keep divine responses rare and meaningful — a god noticing your faith is a story beat, not a buff
- Each PC's relationship to religion should feel distinct; see [[crafting]] and [[faction-leadership]] notes on personal hooks
