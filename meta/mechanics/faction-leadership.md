# Faction & Leadership

Players can build new factions from scratch or influence existing ones. Either path is valid depending on what the story offers. PCs can earn real titles, roles, and glory — not ceremonial ones. Faction state affects the world.

## Overview

- **Encounter type:** social | exploration | any
- **Session fit:** long-term campaign arc; individual faction moves can happen in any session
- **Difficulty:** scalable
- **Player count:** any

## Design Intent

Players want to matter at a political and organizational level. Leadership roles should have mechanical teeth — a title that comes with a seat at the table, real decisions, and real enemies. Building something from scratch vs. climbing inside an existing structure are both valid arcs.

## Paths

- **Build** — founding a new faction: recruit members, establish identity, acquire resources and territory, earn recognition. Starts small; grows through missions.
- **Influence** — working within or against an existing faction to shift its direction, gain standing, or install leadership. Involves internal politics, trust-building, and faction-specific mechanics.

## What Leadership Earns

- Titles that have narrative weight (not just cosmetic)
- Access to faction resources, intelligence, and manpower
- Obligations — factions make demands; leadership has costs
- Enemies — rising in any structure makes rivals

## Relation to World Agency

Faction leadership is one of the highest-expression channels of the [[campaign-design-preferences]] player agency principle. The party's faction choices should visibly reshape regional politics over time.

## DM Notes

- Don't let faction building become administrative — keep it narrative-first; mechanics serve the story
- Titles should come with scenes, not stat blocks
- Other factions should react meaningfully to party ascent
- Each PC should have a faction hook tied to their identity, class, or backstory — leadership opportunities should feel personally relevant, not generic; the right title or role should feel written for that character
