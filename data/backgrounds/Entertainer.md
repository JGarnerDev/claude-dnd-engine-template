## By Popular Demand

You can always find a place to perform, usually in an inn or tavern but possibly with a circus, at a theater, or even in a noble's court. At such a place, you receive free lodging and food of a modest or comfortable standard (depending on the quality of the establishment), as long as you perform each night. In addition, your performance makes you something of a local figure — whenever you are in a place where you have performed, people recognize you.

## Variant: Gladiator

A gladiator is as much an entertainer as any minstrel or circus performer, trained to make the art of combat into a spectacle. You might have fought for a noble's amusement or as a professional combatant in an arena. You can replace the musical instrument with an unusual weapon (net, trident, etc.) as your focus.
