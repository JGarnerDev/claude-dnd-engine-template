**Habitats:** [[Plains]] | [[Dungeon]]

The ankheg is the nightmare beneath every farmer's field — a creature of segmented chitin the color of old rust, its body stretching six feet or more, mandibles capable of shearing through cart axles. It moves through soil the way fish move through water, sensing vibration with organs no naturalist has ever successfully catalogued. What surfaces is rarely the whole creature. Most victims see only the eruption of dirt and the flash of wet, serrated mouthparts before the acid comes.

Ankhegs do not hunt for sport or territory. They hunt because they are always hungry, and the world above the soil is simply a larder they have not yet emptied. Their acid is not venom — it is digestive fluid, secreted while prey is still living, beginning the process of liquefaction before the creature has even stopped struggling. Farmers in contested borderlands have learned to read the signs: circular patches of dead crops, soil that smells faintly of alchemical burn, livestock that vanish without blood or tracks. By the time the signs appear, the nest is already deep.

What makes the ankheg memorable is not its ferocity but its indifference. It does not flee lords or armies. It has no concept of walls or borders or the desperate significance humans attach to the ground above it. Entire harvests have failed because a single ankheg chose to den beneath a field, and no court edict has ever moved one. In lands already stripped by war or famine, their arrival is not a threat — it is a sentence.
