**Habitats:** [[Mountains]] | [[Dungeon]] | [[Cave]]

The behir moves like a landslide given malice: twelve legs churning in unsettling synchrony beneath a scaled body wide as a wagon and long as a ship's keel. Its hide runs slate-blue to storm-grey, each scale edged in faint iridescence where old lightning still crackles beneath the surface. The head is broad and flat, with eyes that catch light like standing water in a cave — pale, cold, patient. When it opens its mouth, the smell of ozone arrives before the sound does.

Behirs are solitary and territorial with the absolute conviction of something that has never needed an ally. They claim mountain corridors, cavern networks, and high passes as sovereign ground, and they enforce that claim without negotiation. They do not hoard wealth. They hoard space. The bones left in their lairs are not trophies — they are evidence of a policy. Travelers, caravans, rival predators, the occasional dragon foolish enough to wander close: all receive the same answer. The behir does not distinguish between them because it does not need to.

What makes encountering one genuinely dangerous beyond its obvious violence is its patience. A behir will wait. It learns the rhythm of a pass, the schedule of a trade route, the hour a patrol relaxes. When it strikes it does so with the economy of something that has never once missed its moment. Survivors — rare, haunted — describe the sensation of the coils as less like being seized and more like a mountain deciding to close around you. The lightning comes after, almost merciful in its finality.
