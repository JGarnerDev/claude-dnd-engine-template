**Habitats:** [[Mountains]]

Its scales catch light like tarnished coins pulled from a long-dry riverbed — greenish-brown along the spine fading to dusty rose at the belly, each plate etched by centuries of wind and the acid that weeps from its jaws when it laughs. And it laughs often. The eyes are amber, deep as a wasp trapped in tree resin, and they watch with the patient amusement of something that has outlived every kingdom whose name you know. There is beauty in it, the way a knife is beautiful.

Copper dragons do not hoard gold. They hoard stories — specifically, the kind that ruin people. A copper dragon knows which lord's heir was fathered by a stablehand, which treaty was signed under duress, which priesthood burned its own temple for the insurance of divine sympathy. It collects these not from malice but from something worse: curiosity, and the quiet pleasure of leverage. It will share what it knows freely, for the right price, which is rarely coin and always something you'll regret.

To meet one on its mountain is to be interviewed. It will test you with riddles that have no right answer, only answers that reveal who you are. It will find your presumptions funny. It will not kill you for insulting it — it will simply remember, and mountains are patient. The danger of a copper dragon is not its breath or its claws, though both are considerable. The danger is that when you leave, it knows more about you than when you arrived, and you will never know what it intends to do with that knowledge, or when.
