**Habitats:** [[Dungeon]] | [[Cave]]

The black pudding is not alive in any way that offers comfort. It is a mass of corrosive, lightless matter that moves with the slow certainty of water finding its level — no eyes, no intent, no malice. It simply is, and where it is, things cease to be. Stone walls weep where it has passed. Iron bars become lace. Leather and wood dissolve in moments, leaving only wet, acrid air and the faint smell of something burned that was never set on fire.

They are found deepest in places that have been forgotten longest — collapsed dungeons, flooded crypts, caverns beneath caverns that haven't seen torchlight in centuries. They do not sleep, do not rest, do not reproduce with any urgency that suggests purpose. They simply expand to fill available space, consuming what they find, contracting when there is nothing left. Scholars who study oozes debate whether the black pudding is more dangerous than other oozes or simply less merciful — most conclude the distinction is academic.

What makes encountering one memorable is the silence. No roar, no hiss, no warning. A torch goes dim against a wall that seems too close, and by then the acid is already working at boot leather, already thinning the air with fumes. Adventurers who survive describe the thing less as a creature than as a decision the darkness made — to become something slightly more deliberate than nothing.
