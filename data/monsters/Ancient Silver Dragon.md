**Habitats:** [[Mountains]]

Aervanthas the Pale Crown has coiled in the high peaks of the Balinok Mountains since before the valley had a name. Its scales, once bright as polished mirrors, have taken on the color of old snow — dull silver-white streaked with grey, each plate the size of a cart wheel and etched by centuries of mountain wind into something almost geological. Its eyes are the pale amber of ancient resin, and they hold a stillness that unsettles even the devout. It does not move like something alive. It moves like weather.

The dragon speaks every language that has died in its presence, and there have been many. It knows the names of lords whose castles are now rubble, the last words of causes that seemed righteous at the time, the precise moment each civilization in this region chose comfort over courage. It does not share this knowledge freely. When it does speak to mortals, it asks questions — quiet, surgical questions that leave the asked feeling as though some essential secret has been exposed and filed away. It will help, sometimes. But its help arrives on its own timeline, shaped by considerations mortals cannot fully perceive, and it has let armies die while it waited for what it determined was the right moment.

To encounter Aervanthas is to be reminded, viscerally, of one's own smallness — not through cruelty but through sheer scale of time. It has seen every moral argument before. Every desperate plea, every righteous cause, every villain who believed they were saving something. The danger of the Ancient Silver is not its breath or its claws, though both will end you. The danger is that it may deem your struggle a lesson the world needs to learn on its own — and watch, patient as stone, as everything you love is lost.
