Common criminal or highway robber. Scimitar and crossbow. Versatile stat block — also used for Vistani raiders, gang members, and desperate villagers turned outlaw.
**Habitats:** [[Plains]] | [[Forest]] | [[Ruins]]

Most bandits wear the same face: hollow cheeks, eyes that move before the head does, hands calloused from something other than honest work. Their gear is a patchwork of violence's residue — a dead soldier's buckle, a merchant's good cloak gone threadbare, a blade nicked from killing something that didn't deserve it. They smell of wet wool and old fire. Up close, they look younger than they should.

They work the forgotten roads, the stretches between towns where the lord's writ thins to nothing. Some answer to a captain, a cause, or a name spoken in fear. Others are simply gravity — opportunists drawn downward by hunger and desperation until theft became the only skill they still owned. They watch travelers the way starving animals watch a fire: from the tree line, patient, calculating odds. The ones who survive long enough get good at reading whether a target will fight or freeze.

What makes them dangerous is not ferocity but familiarity. They were farmers, soldiers, merchants' sons, guildworkers cast out when the guild collapsed. They know how people move, what they carry, when they're tired. An ambush from bandits rarely feels like a monster attack — it feels like a betrayal, like the world's indifference finally given a human face and a knife. And when it's over, whether they run or die or surrender, they leave behind the uncomfortable truth that the difference between them and anyone else is mostly luck and time.
