**Habitats:** [[Mountains]]

From a distance, the creature looks like a trick of mountain light: silver-white scales catching the sun until the whole bulk of it blazes like a second peak. Up close, the scale is wrong in a way the mind resists. Each scale is the size of a shield. The head alone could swallow a draft horse. The cold air around it smells of snow and ozone and something older — the breath of glaciers that no living person has seen. Its eyes are the pale grey of winter sky, and they hold no urgency whatsoever.

Silver dragons are not indifferent the way the wilderness is indifferent. They watch. They remember. They have seen kingdoms rise and collapse into the same soil across timescales that make human history look like an afternoon. A silver dragon may have observed your ancestors three generations back, may have catalogued their failures, may hold opinions about choices made before your grandfather was born. When one speaks, it speaks with that weight behind every word — measured, unhurried, already knowing how this probably ends.

The danger of a silver dragon is not its breath weapon, though the paralytic cold of it can stop a heart. The danger is what happens when it decides your cause is just but your methods are insufficient, or when its long view of history leads it to a conclusion that runs counter to everything you need. It will not lie to you. It will not soften what it knows. And if it judges that the region's suffering serves some larger equilibrium — that the tyrant's reign is preferable to the chaos that follows — it will step aside with perfect courtesy and let events take their natural course. Lawful good, by its own ancient reckoning. By yours, something else entirely.
