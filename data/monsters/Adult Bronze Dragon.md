**Habitats:** [[Coastline]] | [[Mountains]]

Where the cliff-face meets the tide, the rock itself sometimes breathes. Bronze scales carry the color of old coins left too long in salt water — green-brown at the joints, bright copper along the ridgeline of the spine, darkening to near-black at the wing-membrane's edge. The horns sweep back like the prow of a warship. The eyes are pale amber lit from within, and they hold the particular stillness of something that has watched coastlines change over centuries and found the experience merely interesting.

Bronze dragons do not hoard gold. They hoard debts. They insert themselves into wars, dynastic struggles, and maritime disputes — always, they insist, on the side of justice — and they remember every favor rendered, every treaty witnessed, every life bargained for. A captain who once sheltered a bronze dragon disguised as a sailor may find, decades later, that the dragon considers this a binding compact. The dragon will not explain the terms until it wants something. It is lawful good in the way that old law is good: certain of its own righteousness, indifferent to whether its subjects agree.

To encounter one is to feel the full weight of your own smallness — not through malice, but through scale. It speaks to you the way you might speak to a dog you are genuinely fond of. It has seen your kingdom's founding, watched your bloodline thin across generations, and formed opinions about your great-grandmother that it may share at any moment without warning. Whatever crisis you believe you are navigating, the dragon has context that would reframe it entirely, and it will decide for itself whether you are ready to hear it.
