**Habitats:** [[Abyss]] | [[Desert]]

Thirty feet of charred muscle and contempt, the Balor arrives wreathed in fire that burns without fuel and casts no warmth. Its wings are membrane stretched over blackened bone, each beat a sound like a city wall collapsing. The whip in its left hand is braided lightning; the sword in its right hand remembers every wound it has ever made. Its eyes hold no madness — only an ancient, patient hatred that predates the current age of the world by millennia.

Balors do not wander. They are sent. Somewhere in the Abyss, a hierarchy of malice more complex and treacherous than any mortal court dispatched this thing with a purpose, and that purpose is never trivial. They appear at the hinge-points of history — when a city is meant to fall, when a bloodline is meant to end, when something sealed away was never supposed to be found again. The politics of their arrival are invisible to those in their path, which is most of the reason encountering one feels less like combat and more like weather.

What makes them truly terrible is not the fire or the lightning or the sheer mass of destruction they carry with them. It is the certainty. A Balor has never doubted. It does not hesitate, does not negotiate, does not feel the cold creep of fear that every mortal creature knows. When it looks at you — if it looks at you at all — it is performing a calculation, not experiencing a moment. And when it is done, when it is finally brought low by wounds no creature should survive, it does not die quietly: it takes the world around it as payment, detonating in a conflagration that turns the last act of killing it into a question of whether it was worth the cost.
