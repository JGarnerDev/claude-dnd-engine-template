**Habitats:** [[Elemental Planes]] | [[Mountains]]

There is no body to wound, no eyes to meet, no throat to hear a plea. An air elemental manifests as a column of churning wind thirty feet tall, its interior a dim and roaring darkness that tears splinters from wood and blood from skin. Debris circles it like the memory of destruction — dust, leaves, the occasional bone from something it consumed long ago and never thought about again. It does not look like anything. It looks like the space where something should be standing but isn't.

Elementals of air have no agenda, no territory, no hunger in any sense a mortal mind can map. They drift from the Elemental Planes through cracks in the world's fabric — drawn, if anything, by turbulence: storms, high passes, the dying breath of things. Mountain peoples call them 'the deaf ones.' Prayers slide through them. Screaming does nothing. The elemental does not hear the way a creature hears; it is the hearing, the shaking air itself, and it responds only to the logic of wind, which is no logic at all.

What makes an encounter with one harrowing is the scale of its indifference. A warlord is frightening because he wants something. A plague is frightening because it spreads. An air elemental is frightening because you are the same to it as a pine tree or a cliff face — an obstacle, briefly, before the wind moves on. Survivors describe a sound like the world tearing apart at a seam, and then silence, and then realizing they are the only ones left standing.
