**Habitats:** [[Plains]] | [[Jungle]]

The ankylosaurus is armor made flesh. Its back is a mosaic of fused bone plates and bony knobs worn smooth by decades of use, the color of old tallow or river-stone, carrying the dents and scars of things that tried and failed. It moves with the patient weight of something that has no reason to hurry — low to the ground, eyes half-lidded, its breath a slow rasp through nostrils caked with dried mud. The tail drags behind it like a weapon someone forgot to put away.

In the jungle or across open grassland, it grazes with the indifference of a thing that cannot be meaningfully hurt. Predators circle it for the same reason young soldiers circle veteran commanders — hoping to find the angle, learning there is none. The tail does not swing as warning. It swings when the ankylosaurus has already decided, and by then whatever stood too close is already broken. It does not pursue. It has never needed to.

To encounter one in the dark, on a narrow game trail, is to understand in the gut what the old texts mean when they speak of humility before the world. It will not flinch. It will not negotiate. It will continue along its path, and the only question that matters is whether you are in it.
