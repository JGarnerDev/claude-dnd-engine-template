**Habitats:** [[Ruins]] | [[Tomb]] | [[Forest]]

What remains of the dragon is unmistakable in its wrongness. The scales have gone the color of a bruise that never healed, crackling at the edges where the flesh has dried and pulled away from the bone. The eyes are gone — in their sockets, two dim points of cold violet light track movement with an intelligence that has had centuries to become something worse than cunning. Lightning still plays across the ridgeline of its spine, but it no longer smells of a coming storm. It smells of old crypts and scorched earth.

In life, blue dragons demanded tribute and enforced dominion through terror and spectacle. In undeath, it has abandoned spectacle entirely. The dracolich does not roar. It does not circle. It has already decided what will happen, and it waits with the patience of a thing that no longer experiences time as loss. Kingdoms have risen and fallen within its awareness. It has watched bloodlines end. What it has not done — what it is constitutionally incapable of doing — is forgetting a debt or releasing a claim.

To encounter one is to understand, at a visceral and unhelpful level, that you are not the protagonist of the moment you are standing in. The dracolich has larger stories running — older agreements, older enemies, older plans — and your presence is an interruption it will handle efficiently. It may speak. It may offer terms. What it will not do is be impressed, or merciful, or surprised. It has survived the thing that was supposed to end it. Everything since has been politics.
