**Habitats:** [[Desert]] | [[Ruins]]

The desert remembers things that men have forgotten, and so does the brass dragon. Its scales carry the color of sun-bleached bone and old copper, dulled to a warm ruddy sheen by centuries of sand and heat. Scars from old wars cross its flanks — not wounds it was ashamed of, but stories it never bothered to hide. Its eyes hold a brightness that is almost kind, and that is the most unsettling thing about it. You expect indifference from something so old. The warmth is harder to navigate.

Brass dragons do not sleep as other creatures sleep. They sprawl across the ruins they claim and let the centuries move through them like wind. They have watched empires name themselves and unmake themselves. They have spoken with kings whose bones are now dust in kingdoms whose names survive only in their own recollections. When one turns its attention to you — full attention, that vast amber gaze settling on your face like physical weight — the experience is less flattering than it sounds. You are, to it, briefly interesting. A footnote it has not yet written.

The danger of a brass dragon is not its fire. It will talk before it burns you, and it will mean it — the curiosity is genuine, the warmth is genuine, and none of that will save you if you bore it, if you lie to it and it catches you, or if you are simply standing between it and something it has decided to do. It is chaotic good the way a sandstorm is chaotic: the intent is not malicious, but the scale is wrong, and you are very small.
