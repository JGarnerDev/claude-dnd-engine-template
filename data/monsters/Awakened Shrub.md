**Habitats:** [[Forest]] | [[City]]

An awakened shrub looks, at first glance, like any other waist-high tangle of branches and dead leaves. Then it turns. The motion is slow and wrong, like a man trying to remember how limbs work. Its bark-skin is dry and cracked, threaded with veins of something darker than sap. If it has eyes, you do not see them. But it sees you.

These creatures emerge when wild magic or a druid's last desperate act bleeds into the soil and catches in something small and rooted. The awakening gives them hunger and grievance in equal measure. They do not understand why they can think now, or why thinking hurts, or why every creature with legs gets to simply leave. Cities that grow over old forest sometimes find one in a courtyard garden, having stood still for years while the neighborhood rebuilt around it, waiting for someone to make eye contact.

They are not powerful. That is the danger. An awakened shrub can scratch and bite and grab an ankle, but what it really does is make noise — a dry, rattling voice that does not stop, calling out in broken syllables to anything that might answer. In a dungeon, it draws worse things. In a city alley at midnight, it draws guards, or crowds, or whoever was trying not to be found. It cannot be reasoned with, exactly, but it can be bargained with: they want, above all else, to be moved somewhere they chose.
