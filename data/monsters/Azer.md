**Habitats:** [[Elemental Planes]] | [[Mountains]]

An azer looks, at first glance, like a dwarven smith who has stood too close to the forge for too long — broad-shouldered and stocky, with skin like cooled volcanic rock and a beard of actual flame that crackles softly in the dark. Their eyes hold no warmth despite the fire they carry; they are the eyes of a craftsman who has been given an impossible quota and knows it will never be finished. Their armor and weapons are always of impeccable quality, because impeccable quality is the only thing left that belongs to them.

Azers do not wander. They work. They have been working — forging weapons, shaping stone, building structures of terrifying precision — since before the empires of the surface world learned to write. In the Elemental Plane of Fire, they serve the efreet as a conquered people serve a conqueror: with grim competence, silent fury, and a patience that only immortals can afford. Those encountered on the material plane have either been summoned against their will or escaped — and escaped azers are dangerous not because they are cruel, but because they are owed, and they know it.

An azer will not negotiate. It will not banter. If you have something it wants — a tool, a material, a particular secret of metallurgy — it will tell you, plainly, what it will give you in return, and it will expect the transaction to be completed. Cross one, and it does not rage; it simply adds you to a ledger that spans centuries. The most unsettling thing about an azer is not the fire. It is the certainty — the absolute, ancient certainty — that all debts, in time, are settled.
