**Habitats:** [[Gehenna]] | [[Ruins]]

The arcanaloth presents itself as a scholar first and a monster second — robes of deep burgundy and ash-grey, fingers steepled, yellow eyes fixed on you with the patience of something that has watched empires collapse and found the paperwork interesting. Its jackal's muzzle is always faintly smiling. The claws are manicured. Everything about it suggests civilization, which is exactly the point.

They are found in ruins not by accident but by appetite. Where civilizations fell, there are old agreements — debts unpaid, contracts signed by the desperate and the dying — and arcanaloths are archivists of obligation. They do not haunt ruins. They audit them. Ancient halls become offices. Crumbled thrones become negotiating tables. The creature will have already read whatever you came here looking for, and it will know you need it.

The danger of an arcanaloth is not its magic, though the magic is considerable. It is the conversation. It speaks in the register of equals, even when it clearly has the upper hand. It listens with apparent sympathy. It offers solutions to problems you did not know you had. By the time you understand what you agreed to, the ink has dried — and in Gehenna, ink does not fade, and fiends do not forget.
