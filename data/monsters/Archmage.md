**Habitats:** [[City]] | [[Ruins]]

They do not look like weapons. That is the first mistake. An archmage may appear as a bent scholar in a threadbare robe, a silver-haired woman who smells of ink and old stone, a hollow-eyed man whose hands never quite stop moving. The signs are subtle: fingers that leave faint luminescence on whatever they touch, eyes that catch light from sources that aren't there, a stillness in crowded rooms as if the air itself is listening. They have had decades — sometimes centuries — to learn how not to be seen for what they are.

They live in the spaces between institutions: advising councils without belonging to them, cataloguing ruins that no expedition has returned from, corresponding with colleagues who died a generation ago. A city's archmage is rarely its ruler, but rulers rarely make a significant move without consulting one. Their power is not just magical — it is informational. They have been present at enough turning points to understand which lever, applied correctly, topples kingdoms. Most of them have applied that lever at least once. The scar tissue of those decisions is invisible but permanent.

To encounter an archmage who considers you a problem is to understand, viscerally, the difference between being dangerous and being significant. They will not fight you if they can arrange for you to fail on your own. They will not threaten you if a misunderstanding will serve better. And if they do decide to act directly — if you have crossed from inconvenience into threat — there will be no warning, no speech, no theater. Simply the air tightening, the world rearranging itself according to their will, and the quiet, terrible realization that they had decided your fate before you finished opening the door.
