**Habitats:** [[Ruins]] | [[City]]

They come in the plain robes of whatever god claims this corner of the world — faded wool, travel-stained, sometimes mended so many times the original color is a memory. Their faces are unremarkable. That is the point. The institution that shaped them sanded away the edges, the doubts, the private hungers. What remains is someone who will do what they are told by people who will never know their name, in service of a divine will that has not spoken aloud in centuries.

In ruins, you find them cataloguing. Copying inscriptions by torchlight, packing relics into oilskin bundles, following orders from a superior who follows orders from someone else. They are not cruel, necessarily. But they are thorough, and they answer to a hierarchy that extends far above the dungeon you've both wandered into. Killing one brings the question: who sent them, and what were they looking for?

In cities, they are the face of something older and larger — handing out bread at the temple steps, recording births and deaths in ledgers that will outlast the city itself. The danger is not their prayer or their bronze censer. It is that they see you. They take note. They report. Somewhere in a vaulted archive, a clerk adds your description to a file, and the machine of institutional faith begins, very slowly, to turn.
