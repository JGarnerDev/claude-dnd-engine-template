**Habitats:** [[Cave]] | [[Ruins]] | [[Forest]]

A bat is nothing much on its own — a thumb-sized knot of membrane and bone, eyes like seed pearls, mouth perpetually agape as though tasting the air for threats it cannot see. Up close, the face is unsettling: too much ear, too little eye, a snout that seems assembled from leftover parts. They navigate by sound alone, painting the world in echoes, perceiving shapes no torch could reveal.

They roost in the ruins of things that fell long before living memory — collapsed keeps, flooded crypts, the eaves of temples whose names have been deliberately forgotten. A colony can number in the thousands, and when disturbed they erupt outward like smoke made flesh, a shrieking curtain that fills the throat and blots the light. Travelers who've triggered a roost speak of the sound long afterward: not the individual creatures, but the mass of them, a vibration that seems to come from inside the skull.

What makes them linger in the imagination is not their bite, which is nothing, but what they represent — a reminder that the dark places are occupied, that the world's forgotten corners have tenants, and that whatever drove men out of those ruins did not trouble the bats at all. They move freely where armies have died. They winter in vaults that broke kingdoms. To see one flutter across a torchlit hall is to be reminded, quietly, how provisional your claim to safety really is.
