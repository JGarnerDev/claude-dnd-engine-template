**Habitats:** [[Swamp]]

Where lesser creatures leave tracks, an ancient black dragon leaves ruin. Its scales, black as a drowned man's lungs, have calcified over centuries into something closer to stone than flesh — pitted and ridged, trailing long filaments of shed skin like funeral banners. The eyes have gone pale and filmy with age, yet miss nothing; they watch with the cool detachment of a creature that has seen empires reduce themselves to names carved on stones it uses as latrines. Its breath carries the smell of deep swamp water and old blood, and where acid drips from its jaw, the mud blackens and refuses to grow anything for a generation.

Ancient black dragons do not hunt. They accumulate. They settle into a swamp the way rot settles into wood — slowly, completely, until the distinction between the dragon and the place ceases to matter. Local lords may not even know one holds the surrounding wetlands; they only know that travelers who go too deep don't return, that the fish have thinned, that the water tastes wrong. The dragon may have been there longer than the current nation has existed. It finds this amusing, in the way that a millstone finds grain amusing.

What makes them truly dangerous is not the acid or the size or the ancient cunning — it is the indifference. A younger dragon might want something from you: your hoard, your fear, your life. An ancient black dragon wants nothing you have. It will kill you if you are interesting enough to bother with, or let you leave if you are not. Either outcome is equally meaningless to it. There is no negotiating with something that has already decided your entire civilization is a temporary arrangement.
