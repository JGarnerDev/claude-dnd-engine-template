**Habitats:** [[Nine Hells]]

The barbed devil stands roughly the height of a man but shares nothing else with humanity. Its body is a study in engineered cruelty: reddish-black hide erupting in dozens of curved spines along the shoulders, forearms, and crown, each barb hooked at the tip so that anything it seizes cannot pull free without tearing. Its eyes burn a steady amber — not with rage, but with the patient focus of a clerk reviewing paperwork. It does not snarl. It watches. That stillness is the first thing survivors remember.

In the Nine Hells, barbed devils occupy the unglamorous middle of the infernal hierarchy: too low to command, too dangerous to ignore. They serve as enforcers, escorts, and debt-collectors — deployed when a contract has been breached or when a soul already promised to the Hells needs reminding of the terms. They have no interest in temptation or corruption; that work belongs to other fiends. A barbed devil arrives after the deal is done. Its presence in the mortal world means someone, somewhere, made a terrible agreement and has stopped cooperating.

What makes encountering one so unnerving is not the violence — though the violence is considerable — but the impersonality. A barbed devil does not hate its quarry. It bears no grudge, harbors no sadism. It simply has a task and the tools to complete it. It will destroy a man's companions to reach him, burn a village to flush him out, and drag him screaming back to whatever pit awaits, all with the same expression a tradesman wears while settling an account. Afterwards, nothing personal. Just business. The Hells run on business.
