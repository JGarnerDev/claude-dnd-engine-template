**Habitats:** [[Jungle]] | [[Plains]]

The allosaurus does not look like a predator that evolved alongside men. It is too large, too old in its design, a creature whose lineage stretches back to when the land itself was different. Twelve feet of muscle and bone, its forelimbs are short but tipped with curved claws that can rip bark from ancient oaks. The skull is deep and narrow, the jaw hinged wide, and the eyes — set on either side of that long flat head — hold nothing recognizable. No cunning. No cruelty. Only appetite.

In the deep jungles and the wind-scoured plains, the allosaurus moves alone. It does not den with others of its kind. It does not call out or mark territory with sound. It simply walks its range, and the lesser creatures — boars, deer, even the big cats — clear the path or die. Travelers who have survived an encounter speak of how quiet it is. No roar before the charge. The first warning is the ground trembling underfoot, and then it is already upon you.

What makes the allosaurus truly dangerous is not its size or its teeth, though both are formidable. It is the way it reminds you how small civilization is. Standing in its shadow, with your sword feeling suddenly very short and your fire suddenly very small, you understand that the world did not reshape itself for human comfort. The kingdoms and the politics and the wars — all of it exists in a thin layer atop something older and far less interested in your survival.
