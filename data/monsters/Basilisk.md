**Habitats:** [[Dungeon]] | [[Plains]] | [[Desert]]

A squat, eight-legged thing the color of old stone and dried blood, the basilisk moves with the unhurried certainty of a creature that has never needed to run. Its scales are rough and irregular, patched with the dull grey of granite and the yellow-brown of desert clay, as though the earth itself is slowly reclaiming it. The eyes — pale, milky, ringed with something almost luminous — sit wide on a blunt head and rarely blink. They do not glow. They do not burn. They simply see.

Basilisks are territorial but not aggressive in any purposeful way. They eat, they claim ground, they rest in the sun atop the very statues their gaze has made. The petrified figures that dot a basilisk's lair are not trophies. They are furniture. Travelers have stumbled upon entire tableaux — a patrol caught mid-stride, a shepherd with arm outstretched, a child looking back over her shoulder — arranged by nothing more than the indifferent geometry of where the beast chose to walk that morning.

What makes the basilisk terrible is not its power but its patience. It does not rage. It does not pursue. It stands in an archway or crests a ridge at dusk, and whatever meets its gaze simply stops. The stone spreads from the eyes outward, as though the body agrees, one cell at a time, to become part of the landscape. Soldiers who have cleared basilisk dens describe the faces of the statues as the worst part — not frozen in horror, but in the specific blankness of someone who realized too late that understanding the danger and surviving it are not the same thing.
