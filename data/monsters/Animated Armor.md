**Habitats:** [[Dungeon]] | [[Ruins]]

The armor stands in the corridor where it was placed, rust bleeding down its joints like old wounds. The visor is shut. Nothing looks out from behind it. The gauntlets are empty. And yet when you step forward, the halberd rises — not quickly, not with menace, but with the patient inevitability of a drawbridge closing. It does not want anything. It was told to stop you, and so it will.

Animated armors are not monsters in any living sense. They are instructions left in metal — the residue of a commander's last order, a wizard's final precaution, a noble house's refusal to accept that it is gone. The magic that drives them is old and simple: move, block, strike. No fear. No fatigue. No mercy born from recognizing the look in someone's eyes. They last long after the things they protected have crumbled, standing in rooms where the floors have rotted through and the ceiling has half-collapsed, still fulfilling a contract with a world that no longer exists.

What makes them dangerous is not strength but indifference. A man with a sword wants to live. An animated armor has no such complication. It will walk into fire, into a pit, through a wall if it has to — not because it is brave, but because it does not process the option of stopping. The scariest moment is not the first strike. It is the realization, usually a few seconds in, that it will not hesitate, will not flinch, and will not stop until one of you is done.
