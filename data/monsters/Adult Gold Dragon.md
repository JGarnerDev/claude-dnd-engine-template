**Habitats:** [[Plains]] | [[Mountains]]

The scales of an adult gold dragon do not shine so much as burn, each plate a hammered ingot that catches light and holds it hostage. Its eyes are the deep amber of old resin, and behind them moves something that has watched kingdoms rise and rot so many times that it no longer bothers to learn the names of kings. It is enormous in the way that mountains are enormous — not threatening, exactly, but indifferent to your fragility in a way that amounts to the same thing.

Gold dragons are creatures of law older than the laws of any civilization that currently claims the word. They have convictions carved from centuries of observation, and they will share them with you at length. This is not cruelty. They genuinely believe their perspective has been earned. When one settles its gaze on you and speaks your name — and it will know your name — the words arrive with the weight of verdict rather than greeting. It has already decided what you are. The conversation is a courtesy.

What makes encountering one truly dangerous is not the fire or the ancient magic, but the negotiation afterward. Gold dragons do not attack without cause, but their definition of cause is their own, shaped by moral architecture you cannot fully see. They broker deals that span generations, hold debts that outlive the debtor's grandchildren, and remember every promise ever made in their presence. To walk away from one alive is to walk away owing something — even if neither of you knows yet what it is.
