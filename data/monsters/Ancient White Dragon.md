**Habitats:** [[Tundra]]

An ancient white dragon is a thing of terrible, alien beauty. Its scales have thickened over centuries into plates of opaque white-blue ice-stone, cracked along the ridges like a glacier face, permanently rimed with frost that never melts. The eyes are the color of deep polar water — pale, colorless, almost transparent — and they hold no cunning, no cruelty in the way a sentient mind is cruel. They hold only hunger and cold patience. The wings, when spread, blot out the sky like a ceiling of pale leather and hoarfrost, and when the creature exhales, the air does not merely drop in temperature — it becomes a weapon, a wall of annihilating cold that turns stone to brittle ruin and breath to ice crystals in the lungs.

White dragons are the least complex of their kind, which is precisely what makes them so dangerous. They do not scheme. They do not negotiate. They do not collect slaves or hoards out of vanity or pride. They collect because winter collects — indifferently, absolutely. A village's worth of frozen corpses in the treasure pit is simply... accumulation. The dragon does not remember the village's name. It barely remembers killing it. Time moves differently inside a mind that has watched mountain ranges erode.

To encounter an ancient white dragon is to understand what it means to be small. Not outmatched — small. The creature is as old as the ice, and the ice does not hate you, does not particularly want you dead. It simply does not accommodate your survival. Adventurers who have faced younger dragons and won carry hard-won confidence into the encounter. The dragon strips that confidence down to nothing in the first moment of eye contact, when it becomes clear that you are not a threat it needs to take seriously — you are weather. You are a season. You will pass.
