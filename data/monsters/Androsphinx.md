**Habitats:** [[Desert]] | [[Ruins]]

The androsphinx is vast in the way that ruins are vast: not merely large, but old enough that the world has grown small around it. Its lion's body is the color of sandstone bleached by centuries of sun, scarred by wars it witnessed and did not intervene in. Its human face is ageless and unreadable, framed by a mane like a crown of dark iron. When it speaks, the sound arrives before the words, a pressure in the chest that makes smaller creatures want to kneel.

It does not hunt. It waits. Androsphinxes settle at the intersection of what was and what is — collapsed temples, the buried gates of dead cities, places where the earth remembers something men have chosen to forget. They are drawn to significance the way water finds low ground. When travelers stumble into their domain, the sphinx is rarely surprised. It has seen them coming for longer than they know.

To face an androsphinx is to be weighed. It will ask questions — riddles in the ancient tradition, but riddles with teeth: questions about loyalty, sacrifice, the cost of what you seek. Answer wrong and it kills you without anger, the way a stone kills a man who falls on it. Answer right and you receive what you came for, or what you should have come for, which is rarely the same thing. The most dangerous outcome is answering correctly when you do not understand what you've agreed to. The sphinx always does.
