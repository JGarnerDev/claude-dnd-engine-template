**Habitats:** [[Forest]] | [[Mountains]]

The black bear is not large by the standards of the deep wood — not the mountain-breaking bulk of its brown cousin — but it is substantial in the way that hungry things are substantial. Dark fur the color of old charcoal, a blunt muzzle scarred by seasons of rooting through thorns and deadfall, eyes that catch firelight and hold it with unsettling patience. It moves quietly for its size, which is the first thing survivors mention afterward.

They are opportunists by nature, not predators — but the distinction matters less than travelers assume. A bear that has learned human campsites mean food will return to them. A bear that feels cornered, or is protecting cubs, or is simply hungry enough, does not negotiate. It does not posture the way wolves do, does not circle or howl. It commits. The attack is fast and close and involves more weight than most people have ever felt pressing against them.

In the Barovian highlands and the dark hemlock forests south of the village, black bears are a fact of life that the peasants have long since stopped fearing in the way outsiders do. They are simply the land — indifferent, seasonal, following the rhythms of something older than any lord's claim to the territory. What unsettles newcomers is not the bears themselves but the locals' flat calm about them: not bravery, just the weariness of people who have learned that the world does not arrange itself around human concerns.
