**Habitats:** [[Desert]] | [[Plains]]

It emerges from the cracked hardpan of the deep desert like a splinter working its way out of skin — iridescent scales the color of a bruise-dark sky, crackling with static that makes the air taste of copper and scorched sand. The horns are already there, swept back like a crown it was born wearing. Its eyes hold no confusion, no wonder. They hold assessment.

Blue dragon wyrmlings do not wander. They claim. A stretch of badlands, a ruined waystation, a dry riverbed between two villages — it will choose a territory with the deliberateness of a lord reviewing a map and drive out or kill everything that does not serve it. Travelers disappear. Livestock dies strangely. Villagers start leaving offerings at the edge of the flats, half-hoping it will establish terms, because a wyrmling with terms is safer than a wyrmling without them.

What makes encountering one dangerous is not its lightning breath, though that will kill you. It is that the creature is already thinking three moves ahead. It will not fight if it can intimidate; it will not intimidate if it can simply wait for you to reveal your weakness. It is three feet of scale and static and cold patience, and somewhere behind those golden eyes it is practicing the art of domination on you — learning what breaks people, what makes them kneel. When it grows up, it will be very good at it.
