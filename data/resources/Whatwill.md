A glass-like substance of celestial origin, named for the questions it provokes. Reformulated endlessly depending on cultural emphasis: *Whatwill wills, what wills Whatwill?* Shortened to 'Twill where the will is primary; called Watl derogatorily for the wattling done by scavengers scraping leavings after major extractions. It is beautiful when polished cleanly, revered for having fallen from the heavens, and entrancing to gaze upon when the addiction sets in.

## Origin

6.7433 millennia ago, an astral body — [[Astra]] — collided with the planet, gouging a massive [[The Caldera|Caldera]] into the Struck Side of the dwarf planetoid. The impact dislodged many islands on the Arc Side, casting larger Slices into orbit. Smaller Islifts continue to waltz at varying altitudes — some a fathom above ground, others hundreds of fathoms up where the air is thin.

[[Astra]] fused briefly with the planet upon impact, then separated in a pirouette, spinning off trillions of glass shards into its orbital path. The two bodies have since traveled elliptical trajectories, reconvening less impactfully every thousand years — an event called a **Glistening**.

Showerings occur when the planet passes through the band of orbital shards, pulling them gravitically toward the surface. Visual Instruments cannot distinguish the shards from the background Blanket of space. They become luminous and opaque only when rejoined with their mother planet. Predicting where they fall is impossible.

## Arrival

Showerings are heralded by a **Kerning** — a haunting whistle with no reliable warning before it sounds.

Strikes occur roughly weekly. A major landing marks the passing of a **Crawl** (one month), and the calendar reflects this. When a particularly powerful touchdown forms a new Islift, an **Opticon** is called amongst the Lifted, and the Vitrian calendar ticks forward, foretelling the coming of a Glistening.

## Impact Site

The area where glass lands is impossibly illuminated by a Gathering of Light during the Showering. Fallen geodes hum for days afterward, struck like tuning forks by the earth below. Indigenous vegetation is devastated, but a new biome forms — nourished by dust and phosphorescence, dense with alien life, some threateningly carnivorous. Phantasmic entities stalk the resulting **Lensing Zone** — or perhaps it is just a trick of the light.

## Value Denominations

**Dust** — Almost devoid of metallic compound; lowest denomination. Dosed into the body via the eyes, trickling a slurry into the tear ducts. A Thimble buys a modest meal: the leg of a malnourished herbivore, a fistful of Sunchokes.

**Pearl** — Roughly the size of a pinky fingertip. Three pearls secure room and board at a modest inn for a week. A finely crafted blade can be exchanged for a Cloister of ten.

**Disc** — Roughly the diameter of a palm, as thick as a thumb. An Array of four Discs can purchase a family home in a well-to-do urban district. Variation in metallicity and dimension causes fierce price disputes.

**Lode** — Forearm length, rhomboid in profile and cross section, minimum dimensions only. Aureum-quality Lodes can be the size of large furnishings, displayed in precisely lit environments with lenses to focus their light for full effect. **LeyLodes** are the size of a modest home, can have walkable interiors, and have been known to induce full reality fracturings in unprepared onlookers.

## Access & Control

The major landborne powers are relatively isolated from one another, enforced by routine piratical activity. Proximity to the crash site determines who arrives first. Pirates rarely seize troves directly — they prefer to intercept Reclaimants on the return trip to urban sprawls.

Sponsored expeditions by **[[The Partenariats]]** (glass-obsessed merchant consortia) depart quickly from the nearest urban centres, heavily laden with extraction equipment and mercenary bands, with hundreds of hangers-on — Watlers and Dust Scrapers desperate for whatever particle they can seize before high-society agents fill their coffers. Partenariats operate clandestinely, fly no true banner, and are prone to turning coat for a higher bidder.

When a powerful Kerning heralds a titanic LeyLode, [[The Vitricants]] move to enormous lengths to secure and preserve the structure before its own deafening hum shatters it or [[The Mintners]] dissect it into anaesthetic tatters for distribution to bankers.

Urbanites fight in **[[Courts Superior]]** for jurisdiction of landing sites, largely coming to fruitless impasses while deliberation slows activity to a crawl.

It is seen as tragedy when the Islifts are stricken by a Showering — interpreted as Astra falling out of favour with her chosen peoples. The worst outcome: an Islift Stapled back to the ground, forcing its total abandonment.
