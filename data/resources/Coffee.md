Grown only on the volcanic terraces of[[The Ashreaches]] — the ash soil is the missing ingredient, and no other terrain takes. [[Cindermouth Bay]]'s volcanic chain is its natural home. Highland growers call it **ashwake**; the trade grades every legal bean as "black" in House Tomsen's ledger, so merchants just say **the Black**. Smugglers' slang for a late-night cup: **the long candle**. The worthless dust is **grey**.

The brew sharpens focus and holds off sleep — but the alertness is borrowed. Heavy users repay it in waking dreams and shortened lives; the oldest devotees slip into "the long sleep." A dark, oily cup that holds heat unnaturally long, smells across a market, and marks who belongs by how they take it.

## The Five Houses

Each holds one link no rival can replace, and none trusts the rest.

- **House Gilgane** — owns the land. Builds and parcels the grower-settlements on the terraces.
- **House Westane** — owns the plantations, roasting-houses, and the sterilizing step. Every bean is scalded a second time on Westane's order before leaving the highlands, killing its ability to sprout. That one step *is* the monopoly.
- **House Rogarre** — owns the trade routes. Grip locked by an old family compact, now fracturing in a succession feud.
- **House Tomsen** — owns the ledger that grades and prices every legal bean. Quiet, unseen; never touches a sack.
- **House Apostol** — newest and immigrant-risen. Owns the coffeehouses where it is drunk. Wherever Apostol pours, strangers of every class sit, talk, and begin to plot — which makes a nervous crown keep trying to shut those rooms down.

## The Monopoly

Scarcity is a lie. Coffee is not rare — it is sterilized. The houses built their fortunes by killing every seed before sale and burning every wild grove, betting no one would realize it would thrive in any warm valley. The Ashreaches were taken by massacre generations ago; the original growers were wiped out and replaced with bonded labor. House Tomsen's ledger still quietly records it.

House Tomsen burns surplus after good harvests to keep the price brutal. A sealed crate is worth more than a working ox; a sack can buy a title. Merchants carry grams, not sacks.

## Hooks

The rumor that won't die: a smuggler carries seven fertile seeds hidden in a beard. Somewhere a single wild stand the houses failed to burn still grows. Cut the supply — withdrawal is a weapon. Drop the party into an Apostol coffeehouse the night before a crown crackdown, carrying — knowingly or not — the one thing that breaks five fortunes at once: a fertile seed.
