Hand-copied books, loose scrolls, broadsheets, legal documents, and maps. Produced by scribes, monastic copyists, and literate craftspeople. Expensive relative to their weight — a book represents weeks of skilled labor. Common texts (religious tracts, legal forms, merchant ledgers) are cheaper; rare tomes are worth more than most weapons. Literacy shapes who buys them.
