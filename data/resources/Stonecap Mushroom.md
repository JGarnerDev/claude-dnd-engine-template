Stonecap mushrooms grow low to the ground in cool, damp conditions — forest floors, cave mouths, rocky hillsides with good shade. They're not impressive to look at: squat, grey-brown cap, thick stem, about the size of a fist. They turn up in forest, mountain foothills, and the shadowed edges of swamp terrain without much fuss.

They're common enough that no one trades them seriously, but good enough that people who know how to cook them won't settle for something else. Dense texture, earthy flavor that deepens when roasted or braised. Holds up well in pies and alongside heavier meats.

[[The Golden Lioness]] serves them as a house specialty. Where [[Sable Wren]] sources hers is not something she's been asked about yet.
