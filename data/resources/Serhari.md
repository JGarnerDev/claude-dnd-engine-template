Serhari is a low creeping vine found on the plains, unremarkable for most of the year — dusty green leaves, no flower, nothing to distinguish it from ground cover. Once a year, at first light, the entire plant crests in a cascade of yellow, amber, and crimson. By midmorning it has returned to grey-green. That single morning is the harvest window.

The vine grows only within specific elevation bands — high enough for cold nights, low enough for warm days — and concentrates heavily near ancient ruins and sacred sites, likely drawing something from the mineral composition of old stonework in the soil. Outside these conditions, it does not grow.

Plains peoples and their trading partners have known Serhari for generations. It is a cultural asset, not a trade good. What reaches outside markets does so through trusted relationships and carefully managed exchange, not open commerce.

---

## The Three Names

**Serhari** — the plant itself. The name shared by all three preparations in their linguistic root.

**Solhari** — the amber preparation. Dried leaves steeped as tea or added to wines and beers during fermentation. The tea is amber-colored, earthy and slightly sweet with a ginger-like spice. Used communally; marks celebration, gathering, shared occasions.

**Vaelthari** — the white preparation. Seeds ground to a fine powder and thrown into fire, inhaled as smoke. Seeds are harvested only when Serhari seeds — some years it does, some it does not, and no one has determined why. In-world lore holds that the seed is potential, and the plant shares its forward-looking nature with whoever consumes the preparation.

## Effects

### Solhari (Amber)
- **Low dose:** Warmth and social ease — advantage on Persuasion and Performance checks, disadvantage on Insight.
- **Higher dose:** Ecstatic states — ritual dancing, euphoria — plus advantage on saving throws against Frightened.
- **Overdose:** DC 12 Constitution save or Unconscious for 1d4 hours (stable, no lasting harm).

### Vaelthari (White)
Structural logic becomes transparent. Consequence chains extend far forward. The emotional content of what people say becomes static — filtered out automatically, because it interferes with pattern recognition. A practiced mind uses this. An untrained one cannot stop running the implications.

Practitioners call this state the Morath. The name predates the white preparation — it describes the condition itself, not the method of reaching it.

- **Trained users (~20 min duration):** Disadvantage on Insight (emotions register as noise; you see past them to what someone is optimizing for, not what they feel). Advantage on Investigation and Perception. Extra reaction per round. Once per turn, read a creature's combat stance — the DM describes body mechanics, weight distribution, and momentum indicating whether it is about to attack, defend, move, or use an ability (physical prediction, not empathy). Advantage on Intelligence checks to predict consequences of a decision or course of action. After duration: 1 level of Exhaustion (scales with intensity of use).
- **Untrained users:** Same window, but every considered action cascades into consequence chains the user cannot stop running. Disadvantage on Wisdom saving throws for the duration; DM discretion on action paralysis severity.

### the hidden third way
The vine itself, consumed raw during the annual crest. Cannot be prepared in advance; requires being present in the plains at dawn during the cascade. Effect left to DM discretion — possible directions include genuine involuntary prophecy, perception of the land's memory, the Morath, or complete clarity of one's own nature. Masters know this form exists. Most never speak of it.

## Morath Familiarity

DM-tracked, never disclosed directly to players. The character learns something has shifted through fiction — a practitioner's offhand observation, a change in how the Morath feels, a difference in duration or aftermath.

**Tally**
- First use under a plains practitioner's guidance: +2
- Subsequent guided use: +1
- Solo use (any): +1
- Extended time living among practitioners (weeks): +1, once only

**Trained threshold: 5.** Below it, all Vaelthari use follows the untrained block. At 5 and above, the trained block applies.

**Natural talent:** On a character's very first use, the DM may roll 1d20 in secret. On 18+, the character enters the Morath as a trained user immediately. This does not advance the tally — natural aptitude and learned familiarity are distinct.

**Beyond trained:** The tally has no cap. What deeper familiarity unlocks is DM discretion — possible directions include reduced exhaustion, extended duration, safer access to the Unnamed Third, or Morath states that bleed partially into waking life. Masters are not simply trained; they are shaped by accumulated time in the Morath.

---

## Cultivation

Primarily wild harvested. A small number of rare masters actively tend specific patches — protecting them, timing the harvest, reading when Serhari will seed. This knowledge is not widely held and is not written down.

---

## Economy

The real constraint on Serhari is cultural, not economic. Plains peoples do not freely trade it. Solhari surfaces in cosmopolitan trade cities through trusted channels; Vaelthari almost never reaches open markets. A single dose of the white preparation costs what a skilled tradesperson earns in a season when it surfaces at all. Within plains tribes, both preparations are more accessible but still reserved for meaningful occasions — not casual use.

One or two arcane institutions are aware of the white preparation's properties and are actively interested. They have not yet found a way past the cultural gate.

## Story Hooks

- **The institution moves** — A ruthless arcane institution sends agents into plains territory, mapping patches and approaching masters with offers. The party is hired by one side or stumbles into the conflict.
- **Someone gets dosed** — Vaelthari used without consent during a negotiation. Someone read a noble, faction leader, or party member perfectly and manipulated the outcome. There is a trail.
- **Serhari does not seed** — Serhari has not seeded in several years. The deep practitioners are quietly alarmed. Something is disturbing the ancient sites the vine grows near — ruins are being excavated. By whom and why is the question.
- **A patch gets discovered** — The party finds a significant Serhari growth near a ruin — and so does someone else.
- **The crest didn't end** — One patch stayed amber after this year's cascade. Should have returned to grey-green by midmorning. Three days later, still amber. The masters have no name for this. A practitioner has been camped at the site — not harvesting, not guarding, watching. She consumed the Unnamed Third there years ago and has been returning every season since, because what she witnessed during the Morath hasn't finished resolving. She doesn't know if the stuck crest is connected to what she saw. She doesn't know if she caused it. She needs a witness before she takes it again — and she has a reason to ask someone in the party specifically. DM's call on who and why.
